L Laws · Force I

Gravity two ways of seeing it

The weakest force in the universe, yet the one that shapes it. Two of the greatest minds in history gave two completely different answers to what it actually is.

Newton · 1687

A force at a distance

Universal pull between masses

Proportional to mass, inverse square of distance. Every object attracts every other.

It worked — for 200 years

Explained planetary orbits, tides, falling apples. The most successful theory of its era.

The limit

Could not explain Mercury's orbit — or what gravity fundamentally is.

VS
Einstein · 1915

The curvature of spacetime

Not a force, but geometry

Mass bends spacetime. Objects follow geodesics through the curve.

Gravitational lensing

Light bends near massive objects. Confirmed by Arthur Eddington's eclipse expedition, 1919.

Gravitational waves

Spacetime ripples from merging black holes — detected by LIGO, September 14, 2015.

Maxwell · 1865

Unified electricity and magnetism through four equations — and out of the math fell the speed of light. The foundation of all modern physics.

The quantum leap

E = hf

Energy of EM radiation is proportional to frequency. Planck constant h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s. This single equation launched quantum mechanics.

Infinite range

Photons are massless and never decay. Light from a galaxy 13 billion light-years away still reaches us. All astronomy depends on this.

Quarks & gluons

Mediated by gluons. Quarks carry colour charge — red, green, blue — described by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). No quark has ever been found in isolation.

Confinement

Try to rip quarks apart, and the energy you pour in creates new quarks instead. The strong force never lets go.

Stellar fusion

Powers the Sun's proton–proton chain at 15 million °C. Each chain releases 26.7 MeV. That tiny dot is Earth, to scale.

Asymptotic freedom
The strong force behaves backwards: it is weaker when quarks are close, and stronger the further you pull them apart — like an unbreakable elastic band. Nobel Prize in Physics, 2004.

L Laws · Force IV

The weak force — the alchemist.

The only force that changes matter itself — and the forge of every heavy element in existence.

Massive carriers

80 & 91 GeV/c²

The W and Z bosons are extremely heavy — which limits the force to a range of 10⁻¹⁸ m, a thousand times smaller than a proton. Despite the name, it's far stronger than gravity.

Flavour change

The only force in nature that changes the flavour of a quark — converting neutrons into protons. This enables radioactive decay and drives the Sun's proton–proton chain.

Solar dynamo

Plasma flows driven by nuclear fusion heat generate the Sun's magnetic field. Differential rotation stretches and amplifies field lines continuously — the Sun's equator laps its poles.

Magnetic reconnection

Oppositely directed field lines break and violently reconnect, converting magnetic energy directly into kinetic energy and heat. This is the primary driver of solar flares.

Coronal mass ejections

The largest eruptions hurl billions of tons of magnetized plasma into space at millions of km/h. When one is aimed at Earth, it slams into our magnetosphere.

⚠ Geomagnetic storm

March 1989 — Québec goes dark.

A coronal mass ejection struck Earth's magnetosphere, inducing electrical currents in the ground that collapsed Hydro-Québec's power grid in 92 seconds. This is not theoretical — it is the exact scenario shown above.
